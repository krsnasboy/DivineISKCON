Welcome to ISKCON Burla

International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON) Burla is a spiritual community dedicated to practicing and promoting Bhakti Yoga as explained in the Vedic scriptures. Our mission is to spread the teachings of Lord Krishna and help individuals develop their love for God through devotional service.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the temple timings?

The temple is open daily from 4:30 AM to 1:00 PM and 4:00 PM to 8:30 PM. During special festivals, the temple remains open for extended hours.

Is there a dress code for visiting the temple?

We recommend modest clothing that covers shoulders and knees as a sign of respect in the temple environment. Traditional Indian attire is appreciated but not mandatory.

Can I volunteer at ISKCON Burla?

Yes! We welcome volunteers in various departments including kitchen, cleaning, gardening, book distribution, and event organization. Please visit our Seva Opportunities section for more details.

How can I donate to ISKCON Burla?

You can donate online through our website, in person at the temple, or via bank transfer. All donations are tax-deductible and go towards maintaining the temple and its charitable activities.

Spiritual Questions Answered

What is the purpose of life according to Vedic wisdom?

The Vedic scriptures explain that the ultimate purpose of life is to revive our eternal relationship with the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Krishna. This is achieved through the process of bhakti-yoga, or devotional service, which purifies our consciousness and frees us from the cycle of birth and death.

Who is Krishna and why is He worshiped?

Krishna is the Supreme Personality of Godhead, the source of all incarnations and the origin of everything. He is worshiped because He is the supreme enjoyer, the supreme proprietor, and the supreme friend of all living beings. By worshiping Krishna with love and devotion, we fulfill our constitutional position as eternal servants of God.

What is karma and how does it affect us?

Karma refers to the universal law of cause and effect governing all actions. Every action we perform leaves an impression that will bear fruit, either in this life or future lives. Good karma leads to favorable circumstances while bad karma leads to suffering. The goal of spiritual life is to transcend both good and bad karma through devotional service.

What is the difference between the soul and the body?

The soul (atma) is the eternal spiritual spark within each living being, while the body is the temporary material covering. The soul is conscious, eternal, and full of bliss, whereas the body is unconscious, temporary, and subject to birth, disease, old age, and death. Real spiritual progress begins when we identify with our true nature as soul rather than body.

Why do we chant Hare Krishna?

The Hare Krishna maha-mantra is a transcendental sound vibration that connects us directly with Krishna. Chanting cleanses the heart of material desires and awakens our natural love for God. It is considered the most effective spiritual practice for this age, as recommended by the Vedas and great spiritual masters.

What is the significance of vegetarianism in spiritual life?

Vegetarianism promotes non-violence (ahimsa), one of the fundamental principles of spiritual life. By not killing animals for food, we cultivate compassion and purity of consciousness. Additionally, food offered to Krishna with love (prasadam) becomes spiritually nourishing and helps in our advancement in devotional service.

How can one control the mind?

The mind is naturally restless, but it can be controlled through regular spiritual practice. Key methods include chanting Hare Krishna, hearing spiritual knowledge, associating with devotees, and engaging in devotional service. As Krishna states in Bhagavad-gita, "For one who has conquered the mind, the mind is the best of friends; but for one who has failed to do so, the mind will be the greatest enemy."

What is the importance of guru in spiritual life?

The spiritual master is essential because he represents the disciplic succession coming from Krishna. Just as we need a teacher to learn any subject, we need a bona fide guru to understand spiritual knowledge. The guru guides the disciple out of the darkness of material existence into the light of spiritual understanding.

What happens after death?

According to the soul's consciousness at the time of death, it receives another body in the material world or, if perfected, returns to the spiritual world. The Bhagavad-gita explains that just as a person changes clothes, the soul changes bodies. Spiritual practice aims to prepare us for leaving the material body and attaining our eternal spiritual identity.

Why do bad things happen to good people?

From the Vedic perspective, what we perceive as "bad" may actually be the result of our past actions (karma) or an opportunity for spiritual growth. The Supreme Lord arranges everything for our ultimate benefit, even if we can't immediately understand it. By accepting all situations as Krishna's mercy and maintaining our devotion, we transcend dualities of good and bad.

What is the difference between karma, jnana, and bhakti yoga?

Karma yoga is the path of selfless action, jnana yoga is the path of philosophical speculation, and bhakti yoga is the path of loving devotion. While karma and jnana can lead to temporary liberation or material benefits, bhakti yoga leads directly to eternal loving service to Krishna, which is the ultimate goal of all spiritual paths.

Why do we offer food to Krishna before eating?

Offering food to Krishna transforms it into prasadam (the Lord's mercy). This practice acknowledges that everything belongs to God and purifies both the food and the eater. Prasadam nourishes us spiritually and helps reduce material attachments, while also protecting us from karmic reactions associated with eating.

How can I practice spirituality in daily life?

Simple practices include chanting Hare Krishna daily, reading Bhagavad-gita, offering food to Krishna, associating with devotees, and seeing everything as connected to Krishna. The key is to do everything as an offering to Krishna with love. Even routine activities can become spiritual when performed with the right consciousness.

What is the significance of tulasi in devotional service?

Tulasi is a sacred plant beloved by Krishna. Worshiping and caring for tulasi plants brings great spiritual merit. Tulasi leaves are essential for worshiping Krishna, and drinking water that has touched tulasi roots purifies one of sinful reactions. Devotees often wear tulasi neckbeads as a symbol of their devotion to Krishna.

How does bhakti yoga differ from other forms of meditation?

While many meditation techniques aim at emptying the mind or achieving impersonal oneness, bhakti yoga focuses on developing a loving relationship with the Supreme Person, Krishna. It engages all senses in service rather than withdrawing them. Bhakti meditation includes hearing and chanting about Krishna, remembering His activities, and serving Him with love.

Why are the Vedas important?

The Vedas are the original spiritual knowledge given by Krishna at the beginning of creation. They contain comprehensive information about the nature of reality, the self, God, and the relationship between them. The Vedas guide humanity toward spiritual realization through various processes suited to different natures and times.

What is the role of women in spiritual life?

In spiritual life, both men and women are eternal souls with equal potential to develop love for Krishna. The Vedic tradition honors women as mothers and nurturers of spiritual values. Many great female devotees in Vedic history achieved the highest levels of spiritual realization through their pure devotion to Krishna.

How does one progress in spiritual life?

Spiritual progress is measured by one's increasing attachment to Krishna and decreasing attachment to material things. Regular practice of hearing and chanting, following the regulative principles, and serving devotees accelerates progress. The key is to develop sincere faith and enthusiasm in devotional service.

What is the significance of Ekadashi fasting?

Ekadashi (the 11th day after the full and new moons) is especially auspicious for spiritual advancement. Fasting from grains and beans on these days helps control the senses and focus the mind on Krishna. Observing Ekadashi properly can destroy the sinful reactions of eating forbidden foods and greatly please the Lord.
