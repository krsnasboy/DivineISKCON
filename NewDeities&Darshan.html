About Our Deities

The presiding deities of ISKCON Burla are Sri Sri Sita-Ram, Lakshman, and Hanuman. These beautiful forms were installed on [installation date] with great devotion and ceremony, marking a significant milestone in the spiritual landscape of Burla.

According to Vedic scriptures, the deity form of the Lord is not a mere idol but a bona fide manifestation of the Supreme Personality of Godhead who agrees to appear in this form to accept our service and devotion.

The deities are cared for with utmost love and attention by trained pujaris who follow strict standards of deity worship as established by Srila Prabhupada, the founder of ISKCON.

Significance of Deity Worship

Deity worship (arcana) is one of the nine primary processes of devotional service described in the Bhagavata Purana. It helps focus our mind and senses on the transcendental form of the Lord.

  • Provides a tangible focus for our devotion
  • Helps develop love for God through service
  • Purifies our consciousness through direct engagement
  • Allows us to offer our talents and resources to the Lord
  • Creates a sacred atmosphere that benefits all visitors

When we see the deity, we should understand that we are directly seeing the Lord's merciful representation meant for our benefit in this age.

How to Receive the Full Benefit of Darshan

Darshan is not merely seeing the deities but receiving their spiritual glance and blessings. Here's how to make the most of your darshan:

  • Approach with a respectful and humble attitude
  • Offer your heartfelt prayers
  • Chant the holy names quietly (Hare Krishna mantra)
  • Bring an offering of flowers or fruits if possible
  • Stay present in the moment, avoiding distractions
  • Observe the details of the deities' beautiful forms
  • Thank the Lord for this opportunity

Remember that the Lord sees not just our outward actions but the love and sincerity in our hearts.
