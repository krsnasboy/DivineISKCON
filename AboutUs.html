About ISKCON Burla

Discover our spiritual journey, mission, and the divine presence that guides our temple community

Our Humble Beginnings

ISKCON Burla was established in 2010 by a group of dedicated devotees inspired by the teachings of His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada. What began as a small gathering in a rented space has blossomed into a vibrant spiritual center serving the Burla community and beyond.

Our temple was officially inaugurated on the auspicious day of Janmashtami in 2012, with the installation of our beautiful deities Sri Sri Radha Madhava. Since then, we have been continuously expanding our services and outreach programs to spread Krishna consciousness throughout the region.

Our Spiritual Mission

At ISKCON Burla, we are committed to fulfilling Srila Prabhupada's vision of making Krishna consciousness accessible to everyone. Our mission is threefold:

Teach

To systematically propagate spiritual knowledge to society at large and to educate all people in the techniques of spiritual life

Inspire

To inspire devotees to develop love for Krishna and distribute this love to others through devotional service

Serve

To bring members closer together for the purpose of teaching a simpler, more natural way of life centered around Krishna

Our Daily Services

The heart of ISKCON Burla is our daily worship and service to the deities. Our schedule is designed to help devotees maintain constant remembrance of Krishna throughout the day:

  • Mangala Arati: 4:30 AM - The first worship of the day
  • Guru Puja: 7:00 AM - Offering respects to the spiritual master
  • Bhagavad-gita Class: 8:00 AM - Daily spiritual discourse
  • Raj Bhoga Arati: 12:00 PM - Noon offering
  • Sandhya Arati: 6:30 PM - Evening worship
  • Shayana Arati: 8:30 PM - Putting the deities to rest

Every Sunday, we host a grand feast with kirtan, lecture, and prasadam distribution that attracts hundreds of devotees from across the region.
