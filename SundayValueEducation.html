Class Schedule Details

Our Sunday Value Education Classes follow a structured schedule designed to engage children at different age levels while maintaining flexibility for special events and festivals.

Regular Sunday Schedule:

  • 9:30 AM - 10:00 AM: Arrival & Welcome Activities (Coloring, Puzzles, Quiet Games)
  • 10:00 AM - 10:30 AM: Morning Prayer & Bhajans (Devotional Songs)
  • 10:30 AM - 11:00 AM: Main Lesson (Storytelling, Discussion, or Activity)
  • 11:00 AM - 11:20 AM: Arts & Crafts or Drama Activity
  • 11:20 AM - 11:30 AM: Closing Circle & Prasadam Distribution

Special Schedule During Festivals:

During major festivals like Janmashtami, Rama Navami, and Gaura Purnima, we extend our schedule to include special activities:

  • Deity decoration workshops
  • Drama rehearsals and performances
  • Special kirtan sessions
  • Festival preparation activities

Note: There are no classes on major festival days when temple programs run all day. Instead, children participate in the main celebrations.

Age Group Details

Our classes are carefully structured according to age groups to ensure age-appropriate learning and maximum engagement for all children.

Bal Gokul (5-8 years)

  • Focus on simple stories from Krishna's pastimes
  • Basic moral values through storytelling and coloring
  • Short, easy-to-remember mantras and prayers
  • Lots of movement activities and simple crafts
  • Class ratio: 1 teacher per 6 children

Kishore Gokul (9-12 years)

  • Deeper exploration of Bhagavad-gita concepts
  • Discussion-based learning with real-life applications
  • More complex arts projects and drama activities
  • Introduction to scriptural texts and their meanings
  • Opportunities for leadership in younger groups

Tarun Gokul (13-16 years)

  • Philosophical discussions and Q&A sessions
  • Service projects and community involvement
  • Mentoring opportunities for younger children
  • Preparation for youth programs and leadership roles
  • Special workshops on relevant teen topics

Placement: While we generally follow these age guidelines, we assess each child individually to ensure they're in the group where they'll thrive best spiritually and socially.

Our Teaching Methodology

At ISKCON Burla, we've developed a unique approach to spiritual education that combines traditional Vedic teachings with modern educational techniques to engage today's children.

Multi-Sensory Learning

We engage all learning styles through:

  • Visual: Colorful illustrations, deity pictures, and flashcards
  • Auditory: Bhajans, storytelling, and discussions
  • Kinesthetic: Drama, dance, and hands-on activities
  • Tactile: Craft projects and practical service activities

Progressive Learning Model

  • Hear: Stories and teachings from scripture
  • Chant: Mantras and prayers to internalize lessons
  • Discuss: Share understandings and ask questions
  • Apply: Practical activities to implement values
  • Serve: Opportunities to share learning with others

Special Features of Our Approach

  • Positive Reinforcement: Reward systems focused on spiritual growth
  • Personalized Attention: Small group sizes with caring teachers
  • Cultural Connection: Linking ancient wisdom to modern life
  • Parental Involvement: Regular updates and family activities
  • Safe Environment: Physically and emotionally nurturing space

Our teachers undergo regular training in both Vedic content and child development principles to ensure the highest quality spiritual education.

Sunday Value Education Classes

Nurturing young hearts with timeless wisdom from the Bhagavad-gita and Vedic scriptures through engaging activities, stories, and devotional practices.

Class Schedule

Every Sunday from 9:30 AM to 11:30 AM at ISKCON Burla Temple Hall. Special sessions during festivals and holidays.

Age Groups

Classes are divided into three groups: 5-8 years (Bal Gokul), 9-12 years (Kishore Gokul), and 13-16 years (Tarun Gokul).

Teaching Approach

Interactive sessions combining storytelling, arts & crafts, bhajans, drama, and practical life lessons from Vedic wisdom.

Our Curriculum

Bhagavad-gita for Children

  • Simplified teachings from Bhagavad-gita with practical applications
  • Understanding the soul and purpose of life
  • Developing good character and values
  • Learning about Krishna's teachings to Arjuna

Vedic Stories & Puranas

  • Stories of Lord Krishna's pastimes
  • Lessons from Ramayana and Mahabharata
  • Stories of great devotees and saints
  • Understanding dharma through ancient tales

Devotional Practices

  • Learning simple bhajans and mantras
  • Basic Sanskrit slokas with meanings
  • Temple etiquette and deity worship
  • Celebrating festivals with understanding

Value-Based Living

  • Developing compassion and kindness
  • Respect for parents, teachers, and elders
  • Simple yoga and meditation techniques
  • Vegetarianism and caring for nature
